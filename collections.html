Frank Fuller Avery Image Collection

The collection consists of over 800 images selected from 894 5x7 inch glass negatives taken by Avery during the period 1901 to 1916 when Avery was assigned to the Colville Indian Agency.

AWV Bulletins

The Association for Women Veterinarians supported female veterinarians and students from 1947-2012. By the time the organization dissolved, women held a majority of veterinary positions in the U.S. Their bulletin traces the changing emphasis of the organization as the status of women evolved.

The Frank A. Banks Collection

Frank Arthur Banks was a Bureau of Reclamation engineer who supervised the construction of the Owyhee, Grand Coulee and other dams. This collection consists of project histories, ephemeral engineering publications, printed items of restricted circulation, and Banks' correspondence, notes and papers.

William Barkhuff Photographs

The database includes 281 images, mostly photographs, taken by William Delbert Barkhuff during the 1890s. W. D. Barkhuff enrolled at Washington State University on the opening day in 1892 and was the first editor of the college paper, then called the College Record.

Civil Rights Oral History Collection

In February 2001, the Spokesman-Review produced a month-long series of articles titled "Through Spokane's Eyes Moments in Black History," focusing on the civil rights movement of the 1960s. Rebecca Nappi conducted a series of interviews with individuals with ties to both the civil rights movement and to Spokane.

Black Oral History Collection

Interviews conducted by Quintard Taylor and his associates, Charles Ramsay and John Dawkins. They interviewed African American pioneers and their descendants.

Butler Theatre Illustrations Collection

Robert Cushman Butler collected ephemeral theatrical illustrations (primarily engravings and lithographs) relating to popular British and American theatre and music. Over 1000 images from his materials are included here.

Faith Beamer Cooke Papers

Faith Beamer Cooke (1910-2001) was a Washington poet. This collection consists of poems, correspondence, photographs, newspaper clippings, and research materials she gathered for her creative work. These illuminate a little known aspect of women's history and identity in Cold War America.

Digital WSU Football Films

A still growing collection of digitized films from WSU football games, dating from 1916 up to 2005. This is primarily games against the northwest-based and PAC-12 teams.

Philip Dasch World War I Letters

Philip William Dasch left Washington State College to enlist in the Army in 1917. In this collection (78 letters and 10 postcards) of letters to his family, Dasch details his military service, from June, 1917 training at Fort Bliss, TX, to the end of his service in Germany in June 1919.

Early Washington Maps

More than 1250 historic maps presented as high-resolution "zoomable" jpeg2000 files; created as a collaboration between WSU's Manuscripts, Archives, and Special Collections and the University of Washington Libraries.

Government Documents - Western Waters

This collects the full text of 46 government documents relating to the Columbia River, its dams, and associated issues. It was part of the WSU Libraries' participation in the Western Waters Digital Library.

Grays Harbor Photograph Collections

The Grays Harbor region that Pratsch, Girard, and MacKenzie photographed between 1888 and 1921 was a small collection of logging and rail company towns. These images focus on those industries, as well as ship-building and other aspects of daily life.

Homer Hill Materials

63 images from the papers and photographs of Homer M. Hill, a newspaper publisher and community activist in Seattle from the 1880s to 1930s. Topics of note include the 1889 Seattle Fire and Seattle Anti-Chinese Riots in 1890.

George & Frank C. Hirahara Photographs

This collection contains 1,000 photographs related to the incarceration of Japanese Americans at Heart Mountain Relocation Center during World War II. Subjects include landscapes, internees and their families, youth culture, and celebrations and major events within the camp.

Hutchison Studio Photographs

The database includes over 750 photographs taken by Ralph Raymond Hutchison. Hutchison was raised on a farm near Endicott, Washington. As a professional photographer, he later operated studios in Endicott, LaCrosse, Pullman, and Moscow Idaho.

Japanese American Internment Collection

This collection contains materials related to the mass removal of Japanese Americans from the western United States during World War II. These materials include photographs, pamphlets, artifacts, and directories of internees at Heart Mountain Relocation Center.

Paul Kies Autograph Collection

WSU English professor Paul Philemon Kies collected over 400 original manuscripts, primarily letters, signed by prominent American and European writers, monarchs, statesmen, military figures, and performers.

Wallis and Marilyn Kimble Northwest History Database

This database compiles newspaper clippings collected and organized in the late 1930s by the Works Progress Administration to document life in the Pacific Northwest from 1900-1938. Subjects include Bonneville and Grand Coulee Dams, mining, Native Americans, government, the CCC and the WPA, and many more.

Wallis and Marilyn Kimble NW History - Obituary Collection

This collection is a subset of the larger Kimble Collection containing obituaries from Inland Northwest newspapers spanning the period from the early 1920’s through the late 1930’s. Included are death notices for many early Inland Northwest pioneers.

Klondike Photographs Collection

420 images, primarily glass plates from Mart Howard's gold rush experiences in Alaska and the Yukon Territory between around 1895 and 1905.

Clifford R. Koester Collection

228 photographs and documents collected by Koester, an engineer working on the construction of the Grand Coulee Dam. His materials tend to display the working man's point of view on the project, in contrast to the more managerial or official point of view seen in the Frank Arthur Banks collection.

Thomas La Fargue Collection

Photographs and correspondence involving the students of the Chinese Educational Mission founded by Yung Wing in 1872. La Fargue's book about the educational mission, China's First Hundred, was published in 1942.

Frank Matsura Photographs

Frank S. Matsura came to the United States from Japan in the early 1900s, settling briefly in Seattle before crossing the Cascades. In 1907 he moved to Okanogan, Washington, and started work as a professional photographer.

Catherine May Congressional Papers

Selections from the papers of Catherine May Bedell, United States Representative from the Fourth Congressional District of Washington, 1959-1970.

L.V. McWhorter Artifact Collection

Cultural and historical relics of the Yakama, Nez Perce, Bannock, and Flathead tribes, as collected by Yakima rancher L.V. McWhorter.

L.V. McWhorter Photographs Collection

Over 225 photographs and other items from the collection of Lucullus V. McWhorter. The images include portraits of prominent Nez Perce warriors and battlefield sites from the 1877 Nez Perce war.

Moscow-Pullman Daily News Photograph Collection

Over 700 photographs and negatives from the archives of the Moscow-Pullman Daily News, a local paper for Whitman County, Washington and Latah County, Idaho. These images showcase social, political, and agricultural life in the Palouse region from the late 1980s to the early 2000s.

Irwin Nash Photographs

Seattle-based photographer Irwin Nash documented the experiences of Yakima Valley migrant workers and of Yakima and Seattle-based agricultural protest movements in the late 1960s and early 1970s. Over 100 of MASC's 16000+ Nash images are digitized here.

National Park Service Historic Nez Perce Images Collection

Over 1500 images related to the Nez Perce culture. The NPS photographs are housed at the Spalding (ID) Visitor Center/Research Center, Nez Perce National Historical Park.

Nez Perce Photographs Collection

73 images of Nez Perce individuals and groups, most in studio settings, ca. 1877-1905. The images comprise several unrelated sets of photographs.

Northwest College Women's Sports Association

From 1966-1982, the Northwest College Women's Sports Association governed women's athletics in the Pacific Northwest. This collection includes programs and results from the NCWSA and earlier women's sports organizations from the 1940s until 1982.

Northwest Illustrations

This collection consists of 53 selected illustrations taken from the rare 1889 book, History of the Pacific Northwest: Oregon and Washington, Embracing an Account of the Original Discoveries on the Pacific Coast of North America.

Pacific Northwest Missionary Collection

A collection of letters, diaries, and personal writings belonging to some of the most notable missionary figures who served in the northwest during the nineteenth century.

Clyde Pangborn Materials

Clyde Edward Pangborn was one of the great barnstormers of the early 20th century. After an ill-fated "automobile-to-airplane" transfer attempt at Coronado Beach, California in 1920, Pangborn partnered with Ivan R. Gates to form the Gates Flying Circus in 1921.

Propaganda Posters

Approximately 525 Propaganda or War Posters, mostly dating from World War I to World War II. The posters urge the public and soldiers to support the war efforts, from conservation to enlistment to simply watching what they say.

Pullman Tribune

This collects the complete microfilm of the Pullman Tribune newspaper. There are only a few scattered issues from 1895-1909, but the paper is mostly complete from 1910-1919.

Pullman, WSU, & the Palouse Photographs

Several thousand photographs of Pullman, Washington, of Washington State University, and of the Palouse region which surrounds them, selected from an exceptional collection assembled by Ivan Shirrod, as well as the Cities and Towns Collection (PC 2) and other sources.

Mary Martin Rebow Letters

These letters are a rare and significant trove of correspondence of a young woman in Georgian England. Spanning the dates 1767 to 1779, these letters from Mary Martin to her cousin Isaac Martin Rebow document the progression of their relationship from a long secret engagement to married life.

George Ritchey Image Collection

Amateur photographer George Ritchey lived in Pullman at least as early as 1897. He worked in a Pullman grain elevator in the 1890s; by 1910, he was a house painter living on Military Hill with his wife and children.

Scrapbook of Taiwan under Japanese Occupation

A single volume scrapbook of 80 images taken during Hubert Adams' travels in Taiwan in 1935. Photographs depict lumbering, fishing, sugar refining, public works, shrines, villages, and other topics.

Steamboat Photographs

Found in a trunk by a resident of Richland, seven of the eighteen photographs in this collection appeared in Tri-City Herald on April 3rd, 1979. They were taken (or at least printed) by the Wilkin Photo Service Lewiston, Idaho.

Toppenish Roundup Photographs

80 photographs from H.E. Maris of the 1914 Toppenish Roundup. Includes images of Native Americans, regional cowboys, and period entertainments.

Washington Women's History, 1879-1950

A collection of documents from Manuscripts, Archives, and Special Collections holding special relevance to the history of women in Washington State.

Cull White Photo Collection

Cull A. White collected photographs of the history of the peoples of north-central Washington. His 260 images focus on the lives of Native Americans and the lives of pioneer settlers.

Whitman County Historical Society Oral Histories

111 interviews totaling 217 hours of audio, conducted between 1978 and 1984. They interviewed surviving pioneers from the early days of non-native settlement in Whitman County.

Women at WSU

Women from Washington State University and the surrounding community are the primary focus of over 600 photographs, newspaper clippings, scrapbook items, and printed ephemera. Includes women students in the military, theater and dance, home economics, sororities, clubs, and other organizations.

Women in the West Oral Histories

The 197 oral histories in this collection were conducted by WSU students between 1970 and 2005, and document the evolving role of women in the Pacific Northwest.

World Civilizations Repository

This site, the World Civilizations Image Repository (WCIR), consists of a series of image databases drawn from donated personal faculty collections and from images located in Manuscripts, Archives, and Special Collections (MASC) at the WSU Libraries.

World War I Cartoons Collection

41 American political cartoons related to U.S. involvement in World War I. Selected from magazines and other publications of the time.

WSU Alumni Newsletters

The Powwow, later Hilltopics, was an alumni newsletter published from 1910 to 2001, and documents campus events as well as the lives of our students and graduates. The complete run covers 650 issues.

WSU Board of Regents Minutes

Minutes of WSU's governing body, the Board of Regents, from their first meeting on April 22, 1891 up to present day.

WSU Buildings Photographs

The historical photographs which comprise the WSU Buildings Image Database were selected from various MASC collections and document the evolution of the Pullman WSU campus and the existence of some of the since-destroyed campus buildings and features.

WSU College of Nursing Materials

Papers and photographs documenting the evolution of nursing education in the Pacific Northwest, primarily focused around Washington State University, the coalitions they were part of, and their partner institutions.

WSU Departmental Histories

This still-growing collection contains more than 50 Washington State University campus histories, departmental histories, and other WSU-specific histories. Most of the items in this collection are secondary historical works.

WSU Employee Newspapers

From 1971-2009, WSU published faculty/staff newspapers as a primary means of communication to employees. WSU Today, WSU Week, University Bulletin, and Bulletin/Calendar are all collected here, over 1100 issues.

WSU Extension Series

Beginning in 1913, the Extension Station published several parallel series of Extension Publications (Bulletins, Circular, Mimeographs, and more) as a form of educational outreach to the farmers and home-makers of Washington. This collection is incomplete and still growing.

WSU Flu Epidemic of 1918

A collection of materials from the papers of WSC President E.O. Holland and the WSU University Publications collection related to the 1918 Influenza Epidemic at the State College of Washington (now WSU).

WSU Football Programs

This collection contains approximately 225 Washington State University football programs, dating as far back as 1906.

WSU Graduation Brochures

This collection contains brochures, speeches, invitations, and calendars from Washington State University commencements, baccalaureates, or other graduation-related events which occurred between 1892 and present day.

WSU Libraries' Films

Washington State University has produced numerous formal and informal films, a portion of which, dating from 1916 to present, have been digitized here, documenting life on campus, in the Pullman and the Palouse area, and in the greater Pacific Northwest.

WSU Oral Histories

In April 2004 the Golden Grad Class of 1954 commemorated their graduation from Washington State University (then Washington State College) by establishing a fund to create the WSU Oral History Project (OHP).

WSU Photo Services Photographs

Images available only on the WSU Pullman campus. Images of student life, activities, and events on campus, as taken by WSU's Photo Services.

WSU Student Newspapers (Evergreens)

WSU has had a student newspaper, the Evergreen, since almost the first days of the school. This collects the full run of student papers, over 13,000 issues, from 1892 to the end of last year.

WSU Student Unrest of the '60s and '70s

Approximately 100 printed items - leaflets, handbills, university documents, newspaper clippings and other items dating from student activism at Washington State University in the late 1960s and early 1970s.

WSU Vancouver Digital Collections

Digital Collections created and maintained by WSU Vancouver covering Clark County, Columbia Basin and Washington State history.

WSU Yearbooks (Chinooks)

WSU's yearbook, the Chinook, was first published in 1899 and continues today. This collection runs from 1899 to 1986, 87 volumes and 37992 pages.
