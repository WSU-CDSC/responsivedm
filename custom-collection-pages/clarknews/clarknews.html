Welcome to the Clark County Historical Museum Newspapers Archive

The Clark County Historical Museum Newspapers Archive is a collaborative project between the Clark County Historical Museum and the Washington State University Vancouver Library. The current featured collection includes issues of The Vancouver Independent, one of the first newspapers of the Washington Territory published in Clark County. Issues for this collection cover a range from January 1876 to December 1888 . The project is ongoing. Check back often for more additions. About the Vancouver Independent

The Vancouver Independent was one of the first newspapers of the Washington Territory, covering news of Vancouver, Washington and the southern counties. It began publishing weekly in 1875. In its formative years, the Independent was distributed on Saturdays. In 1877 it changed its format from a four page paper to an eight page weekly delivered on Thursdays. The newspaper was established by William B. Daniels. Although the Independent aspired to be a politically neutral source, Daniels was active in the Republican Party. Daniels latter became mayor of Vancouver and then served in the state House of Representatives in 1898. Daniels sold the newspaper in 1878 to Thurston Daniels and John J. Beeson. The Independent ceased publication in 1910 under the ownership of Lloyd DuBois.
