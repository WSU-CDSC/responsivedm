Project Team

Leslie Wykoff, M.L.S.

Project Co-Director

Leslie is the Director of Information Services at Washington State University Vancouver. Vancouver's Information Services includes the campus library, information technology, and telecommunications. Leslie is member of the WSU library faculty and does research in the field of integrated information services. She spent many years as a medical librarian working for the Oregon Health Sciences University and the Kaiser Center for Health Research.

Laurie Mercier, Ph.D.

Project Co-Director

Mercier is Associate Professor of History at WSUV and teaches courses on Modern America, the Pacific Northwest, Oral History, and Immigration and Migration. She has co-directed many public history projects that have included several web sites and the Idaho Ethnic Heritage Project. She is the author of numerous publications about women's, labor, and community history including the recent Anaconda: Labor, Community, and Culture in Montana's Smelter City (University of Illinois Press, Working Class in American History Series, 2001).

Robert Schimelpfenig, MA

Research Associate

Robert has worked for the Reference Department at the Washington State University Vancouver Library for the past six years. During that time he has been involved in a number of projects relating to library research including recent participation in the library's Virtual Reference Desk. Robert has a background in history and has worked with the Project's Co-Director, Laurie Mercier, on the Columbia Communities Moses Lake project for the Center for Columbia River History.

Jeannette Altman, B.A.

Principal Artist/Designer

Jeannette is the studio tech for the Fine Art complement of the Digital Technology and Culture program at Washington State University Vancouver. She has worked on many multimedia and web-based projects, including the educational DVD title Red Planet: Scientific and Cultural Encounters with Mars as lead artist. Upcoming projects include Futurama, Autogeddon with Dr. Helen Burgess and Dr. Jeanne Hamming. Her interests include photography, printmaking, design, and sculpture.

Mario Compean, M.A.

Research Coordinator

Mario Compean was a member of the faculty at Yakima Valley Community College from 1997 to 2001, where he served as Instructor and Coordinator of Chicana/o Studies. In 2000 at Yakima Valley Community College he coordinated research for the Crewport History Project, which conducted oral history interviews with residents of the Crewport Farm Labor Camp in the Yakima Valley. Currently Mario Compean is conducting research with Mexican and other Latin American immigrants in southwestern Washington, and is engaged in graduate studies in History at Washington State University, with emphasis on the history of Mexicans and Mexican Americans in the Pacific Northwest.

Nicole Campbell, M.L.S.

Research and Development

Nicole Campbell is a Reference Librarian and library faculty member at WSU Vancouver. In addition to providing reference and instructional services, she also serves as the library's webmaster and coordinator of electronic resources. Nicole's research interests include assessing the usability of web-based resources to help ensure that these resources are both useful and usable. Her research has included numerous focus group session and usability studies.

Trevor James Bond, M.L.I.S., M.A.

Research and Development

Trevor Bond is Special Collections Librarian for Manuscripts, Archives, and Special Collections where he oversees the printed and visual collections. He also teaches GenEd 110, World Civilizations, through the Department of General Education and serves as the Library Subject Specialist for Classical Literature.


Alan Cornish, M.L.S.

Research and Development

Alan Cornish is a Systems Librarian at the WSU Libraries in Pullman, Washington. He supports digital collection building and repository efforts for the Libraries. Alan administers the DiMeMa CONTENTdm and the RealNetworks Helix servers used to support the Columbia River Basin Ethnic History Project. He also works as a reference librarian at the Owen Science and Engineering Library.
